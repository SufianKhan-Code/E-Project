Mountaineering Records

Record Achievement Date Climber(s) Significance
FIRST ASCENT Mount Everest May 29, 1953 Sir Edmund Hillary & Tenzing Norgay First confirmed ascent of world's highest peak
14 PEAKS All 8000m Peaks 1986 Reinhold Messner First to climb all 14 eight-thousanders without oxygen
YOUNGEST Everest Summit May 22, 2010 Jordan Romero (13 years) Youngest person to summit Everest
OLDEST Everest Summit May 23, 2013 Yuichiro Miura (80 years) Oldest person to summit Everest
SPEED RECORD Everest Ascent May 21, 2004 Pemba Dorje Sherpa Fastest Everest ascent: 8h 10min from Base Camp
WOMEN'S FIRST Everest Summit May 16, 1975 Junko Tabei First woman to summit Everest
WINTER ASCENT K2 Winter Summit January 16, 2021 Nirmal Purja & Team First winter ascent of the world's 2nd highest peak
SEVEN SUMMITS Fastest Completion 2019 Kristin Harila Fastest woman to complete Seven Summits (6 months)
HISTORIC FIRST

Everest 1953

The first confirmed ascent of Mount Everest marked a milestone in human exploration and opened the door to high-altitude mountaineering.

May 29, 1953
Hillary & Tenzing
ULTIMATE CHALLENGE

14 Eight-Thousanders

Climbing all 14 peaks above 8000 meters is considered the ultimate mountaineering challenge, achieved by only a select few.

First: Reinhold Messner
Completed: 1986
SPEED DEMON

Fastest Everest Ascent

Pemba Dorje Sherpa's record of 8 hours 10 minutes from South Base Camp to summit remains one of the most impressive speed records.

8h 10min
May 21, 2004
Record Evolution

Mountaineering records continue to evolve with advancements in equipment, training, and climbing techniques. What was once considered impossible is now regularly achieved by dedicated climbers.

Safety First

While records inspire and motivate, safety should always be the top priority. Many record attempts have ended tragically when climbers pushed beyond safe limits.
